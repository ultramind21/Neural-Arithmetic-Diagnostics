## 1. Paper Identity

### Paper Target
The first paper should be built primarily from **Project 4**.

---

## 2. Working Title

**When High Arithmetic Accuracy Is Not Enough: A Diagnostic Framework for Structural Robustness in Neural Arithmetic**

This remains the current preferred title unless a shorter conference-facing version is later needed.

---

## 3. Core Scientific Claim

The paper should make one main claim clearly:

> High arithmetic performance alone is not sufficient evidence of robust reasoning, and structured diagnostics are necessary to distinguish narrow gains from broader structural robustness.

This is the paper's center of gravity.

---

### Must include
- Project 4 framework
- regime definitions
- scorecard dimensions
- stable baseline matrix
- first intervention result (adversarial training)
- bounded interpretation
- explicit limitations

### May mention briefly
- audit background as motivation

### Should NOT try to include deeply
- full Project 5, 6, and 7 development
- large mechanistic arcs
- all later architecture explorations

Those belong to later papers.

## 5. What the Paper Should Exclude

To keep Paper 1 clean, it should avoid becoming:

- a full audit paper
- a complete historical retrospective
- a mechanistic interpretability paper
- a decomposition theory paper
- a local-to-global bridge paper

Those are valuable directions, but not the center of Paper 1.

## 6. Strongest Assets for Paper 1

The strongest assets that make Project 4 paper-ready are:

1. a clear problem statement
2. a reusable framework
3. regime-based interpretation
4. stable baseline matrix
5. a first intervention showing narrow gain without broad transfer
6. a strong methodological message that generalizes beyond one model result
