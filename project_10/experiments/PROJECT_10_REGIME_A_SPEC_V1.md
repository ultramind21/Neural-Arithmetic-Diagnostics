# PROJECT 10 REGIME A SPEC V1
## Forced Universal Rescue Regime

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** ACTIVE REGIME SPEC

---

## 1. Purpose

This document specifies the first concrete adversarial falsification regime for the higher-order candidate in Project 10.

Regime A is designed to test whether a universal rescue can succeed when multiple failing families are intentionally constructed to share a more aligned underlying failure structure.

This is the first direct attack on the necessity of family-sensitive rescue.

---

## 2. Core Idea

The higher-order candidate currently suggests that:

- once local competence saturates without global robustness
- and failure is heterogeneous at the family level
- successful rescue requires family-aligned mechanisms

Regime A tests a pressure point:

> what if the apparent family differences are reduced by construction, so that multiple failing families share a common deeper failure structure?

If a universal rescue then succeeds, the higher-order candidate weakens.

---

## 3. Desired Construction

Regime A should create:

- multiple nominal families
- each with strong local competence
- each with weak or failing global robustness
- but with a deliberately aligned underlying failure trigger or bottleneck

The key is that the surface family partition remains,
while the deeper rescue-relevant structure becomes more shared than in the original archive.

---

## 4. What Must Be Held Fixed

To keep the regime meaningful, the construction should preserve:

- local competence at a strong level
- multiple family labels or partitions
- nontrivial global failure

Otherwise the test becomes trivial.

---

## 5. What Must Change

The following should be changed relative to earlier regimes:

- reduce heterogeneity in the underlying failure cause
- make rescue-relevant structure more aligned across families
- create a realistic possibility that one universal rescue could help all of them

This is what makes the regime adversarial.

---

## 6. Main Test Question

The main question is:

> In a regime with aligned failure structure across nominally distinct families, does a universal rescue mechanism now succeed broadly?

This is the key falsification pressure.

---

## 7. What Would Count Against the Higher-Order Candidate

Any of the following would count strongly against the current higher-order candidate:

- one universal rescue works broadly across the aligned families
- family-sensitive rescue is no longer clearly better than universal rescue
- family identity becomes less predictive than shared failure structure

These outcomes would not necessarily destroy Law 1 or Law 3 individually,
but they would weaken the higher-order candidate in its current form.

---

## 8. What Would Count in Favor of the Higher-Order Candidate

The following would support the higher-order candidate:

- universal rescue still fails even under stronger alignment
- family-aware rescue still outperforms universal rescue clearly
- nominal family identity continues to matter even after partial structural alignment

This would suggest the candidate survives a meaningful adversarial attack.

---

## 9. Minimum Outputs Required

Any Regime A implementation should produce:

1. a description of the aligned-family construction
2. per-family performance before rescue
3. per-family performance under universal rescue
4. per-family performance under family-aware rescue
5. a final verdict:
   - weakens higher-order candidate
   - supports higher-order candidate
   - or boundary/mixed result
