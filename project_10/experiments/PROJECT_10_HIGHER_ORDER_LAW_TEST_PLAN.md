---

## Target Candidate

### Higher-Order Candidate
**When local competence reaches saturation without global robustness, successful rescue requires mechanisms aligned to the heterogeneous structure of family-level failure.**

This is the first higher-order law candidate in Project 10.

---

## Why This Candidate Is Important

This candidate attempts to unify:

- **Law 1**  
  Local competence is not sufficient for global robustness

- **Law 3**  
  Rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal

If this candidate survives testing, then the research line is no longer just accumulating laws.
It begins to form a layered theory.

---

## What Must Be Tested

The test must ask whether the current program actually supports the full higher-order chain:

1. local competence saturates
2. global robustness still fails
3. family-level failure is heterogeneous
4. rescue becomes family-sensitive rather than universal

If any major part of that chain fails, the higher-order candidate weakens.

---

## Evidence Needed

Relevant evidence should come from:

- Project 5 decomposition findings
- Project 6 mechanistic findings
- Project 7 local-to-global heterogeneity findings
- Project 8 family-sensitive architecture rescue
- optionally Project 9 if the higher-dimensional evidence is structurally relevant

The test should not depend on one project alone.

---

## Desired Outcomes

### Strong support outcome
The program shows that:
- local competence saturation is real
- family-level failure heterogeneity is real
- rescue shifts toward family-sensitive architecture support
- and these elements form a coherent causal/structural chain

### Partial support outcome
Some parts of the chain are well-supported, but one layer remains weaker or more conditional.

### Weak or failed outcome
The chain does not hold together cleanly, or key components remain unsupported.

All three outcomes are scientifically useful.

---

## What Would Count Against the Candidate

The higher-order candidate would be weakened by results showing any of the following:

- broad global robustness from local competence without family-sensitive support
- universal rescue mechanisms working across families without meaningful differentiation
- no stable family-level heterogeneity in the relevant branches
- evidence that the relationship between Law 1 and Law 3 is weaker than currently believed

---

## Methodological Rule

This test should be stronger than the first law tests.

It must:
- use structured evidence
- preserve boundaries explicitly
- separate support from convenience
- and avoid treating suggestive synthesis as theory

This is especially important because higher-order law candidates are easier to overstate than lower-order ones.
