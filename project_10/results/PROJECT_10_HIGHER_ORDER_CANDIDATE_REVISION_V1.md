# PROJECT 10 HIGHER-ORDER CANDIDATE REVISION V1
## Narrower Reformulation After Adversarial Pressure

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** ACTIVE THEORY REVISION

---

## 1. Why This Revision Exists

The original higher-order candidate was too strong in its necessity form.

Adversarial testing showed that:

- family-aware rescue can outperform universal rescue under important baseline conditions
- but this advantage can shrink sharply under deep family homogeneity
- and can reverse under sufficiently amplified universal rescue power

This means the stronger version of the candidate does not survive unchanged.

A narrower reformulation is now required.

---

## 2. Original Candidate

The original higher-order candidate was:

> When local competence reaches saturation without global robustness, successful rescue requires mechanisms aligned to the heterogeneous structure of family-level failure.

This formulation is now too strong if read as a broad necessity claim.

---

## 3. Revised Candidate V1

### Revised working statement
**When local competence saturates without global robustness, family-aligned rescue tends to outperform universal rescue when family-level failure heterogeneity is substantive and universal mechanism power is not sufficient to override that heterogeneity.**

This is the revised higher-order candidate.

---

## 4. What Changed

The key changes are:

### A. from "requires"
to
### "tends to outperform"

This removes the unjustified necessity claim.

---

### B. from broad family sensitivity
to
### substantive family-level heterogeneity

This reflects the pressure from Regime B.

---

### C. from unconditional rescue structure
to
### rescue performance relative to mechanism power

This reflects the pressure from Regime C.

---

## 5. Why This Revision Is Better

This revision is better because it preserves what actually survived:

- family-aware rescue can matter
- family-aware rescue can outperform universal rescue
- but not under all regimes
- and not as a strict necessity

It converts an overstated higher-order claim into a bounded comparative principle.

---

## 6. What This Revised Candidate Predicts

If the revised candidate is correct, then:

- under substantive family heterogeneity and baseline rescue power,
  family-aware rescue should retain an advantage

- under deep homogenization of failure structure,
  that advantage should shrink or disappear

- under sufficiently amplified universal rescue power,
  family-aware advantage may vanish or reverse

These predictions are now aligned with the adversarial findings.

---

## 7. Current Status

This revised candidate is:

- more defensible than the original
- still in need of structured testing
- and not yet established as a strong law

Its current role is:
- revised higher-order hypothesis
- not final accepted principle
