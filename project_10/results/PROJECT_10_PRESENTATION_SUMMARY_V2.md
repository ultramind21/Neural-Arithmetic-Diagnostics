## Project 10 in One Paragraph

Project 10 began as a theory-building effort aimed at extracting compositional failure laws from the broader research program. It established strong support for Law 1 — that local competence is not sufficient for global robustness — and strong but bounded support for Law 3 — that rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal. It then attempted a stronger higher-order unification, but adversarial testing showed that this unification did not survive in its original necessity form. A narrower revision also proved too broad as a flat principle. The strongest surviving higher-level result was therefore not a single higher-order law, but a threshold-structured regime-space account in which family-aware advantage, mixed behavior, and universal dominance occupy different regions, and the boundary between them shifts with heterogeneity.

---

## Core Scientific Outcome

The central contribution of Project 10 is not that it found one clean universal top-level law.

It is that it showed, under explicit testing and adversarial pressure, that higher-level rescue behavior is better described through:

- bounded law structure
- regime-space dependence
- threshold-like transitions
- and heterogeneity-dependent boundary geometry

This is the strongest current Project 10 position.

Project 10 matters because it did not stop at:
- project-level findings
- or attractive high-level claims

Instead, it:
- extracted candidate laws
- tested them structurally
- exposed where higher-order claims were too strong
- and replaced them with a more defensible theory of rescue regimes and transition structure

That makes it one of the most theoretically mature branches in the repository.

---

## Final Position

Project 10 shows that robust rescue behavior in compositional systems may resist compression into a single universal higher-order law. A more accurate description is that rescue behavior depends on where the system lies in a structured regime space shaped at least by family-level heterogeneity and universal rescue power.
