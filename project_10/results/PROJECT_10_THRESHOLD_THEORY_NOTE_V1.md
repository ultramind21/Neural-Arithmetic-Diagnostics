## 1. Purpose

This note records the strongest higher-level theoretical insight that has emerged from Phase 2 of Project 10 so far.

The goal is to state clearly what the current evidence now suggests beyond:
- the original higher-order candidate
- and beyond its revised V1 form

The new insight is that rescue behavior appears to be threshold-structured.

---

## 2. Main Theoretical Insight

The strongest current insight is:

> comparative rescue behavior is not best described as a globally stable law, but as a threshold-governed phenomenon in regime space.

This is the central statement of the current note.

---

## 3. What This Means

This means that:

- family-aware rescue does not simply "win"
- universal rescue does not simply "fail"
- and rescue behavior is not controlled by one flat necessity rule

Instead, the comparative outcome depends on whether the system lies:
- below
- near
- or above

certain transition regions in regime space.

This is a stronger and more precise interpretation.

---

## 4. Current Threshold Dimensions

The current evidence supports at least two threshold-relevant dimensions:

### A. Family-level heterogeneity
A transition region appears between low and medium residual heterogeneity.

### B. Universal rescue power
A transition region appears between baseline and moderate universal rescue power.

These dimensions jointly shape the comparative outcome.

---

## 5. Current Best Reading of the Evidence

The evidence now suggests the following structure:

- below a heterogeneity threshold, family-aware rescue has little or no clear advantage
- above that threshold, family-aware rescue can gain a clear advantage
- but increasing universal rescue power can erase or reverse that advantage
- therefore rescue dominance depends on threshold position, not on one simple universal principle

This is the current best reading.

---

## 6. Why This Is Stronger Than the Earlier Higher-Order Candidate

This threshold view is stronger because it:

- matches both the coarse matrix and the local refinement probe
- explains why the original higher-order candidate became too strong
- explains why the revised candidate still remained too broad
- and captures the fact that transitions matter more than broad necessity language

This makes it a more defensible theory direction.
