# PROJECT 10 SYNTHESIS V1
## Compositional Failure Laws — First Theory Position

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** ACTIVE SYNTHESIS  
**Scope:** first synthesis, not final closure

---

## 1. Purpose

This document records the first synthesis point of Project 10.

Project 10 was launched to move the research program from:
- results
- patterns
- and project-level findings

toward:
- compact, explicit, testable laws

The project has now produced its first two strongly supported candidate laws.

That is enough to justify a first theory-level synthesis.

---

## 2. What Project 10 Has Achieved So Far

Project 10 did not begin by assuming theory.
It began by asking whether the strongest patterns from Projects 4–9 could survive structured law testing.

The answer, so far, is yes.

Two candidate laws have now reached **strong support**:

### Law 1
**Local competence is not sufficient for global robustness.**

### Law 3
**Rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal.**

These are now the first two serious theory-level results in the program.

---

## 3. Why These Two Laws Matter

These two laws are not independent curiosities.
They are deeply connected.

### Law 1 says:
- even strong local competence does not guarantee global robust composition

### Law 3 says:
- rescue mechanisms that attempt to fix global failure are themselves family-sensitive rather than universal

Together, they imply something stronger:

> the local-to-global compositional problem is not solved either by local competence alone or by one universal rescue mechanism.

This is a major advance over earlier, simpler views of arithmetic reasoning failure.

---

## 4. The Strongest Current Theory Position

The strongest current Project 10 theory position is:

> global robust compositional behavior does not follow automatically from local competence, and the mechanisms that can rescue failure are themselves structured, family-sensitive, and non-universal.

This is the strongest compact statement currently supported by the program.

---

## 5. Why This Is a Real Theory Step

This matters because the research line has now moved beyond:
- "interesting results"
- "strong project findings"
- or "one more experiment"

It now contains:
- explicit candidate laws
- strong support criteria
- and a methodology for trying to falsify them

That is the first moment where the program begins to look like a real theory-building effort rather than a chain of empirical discoveries alone.

---

## 6. What Law 1 Organizes

Law 1 organizes a large portion of the research line:

- Project 5 local decomposition results
- local bottleneck findings
- explicit carry-conditioned rescue limits
- Project 7 local-to-global bridge failures
- Project 8 architecture-level need for additional support

Its role is to explain why:
- local success
- local representational clarity
- and local causal importance

still do not automatically produce strong global compositional behavior.

This makes Law 1 one of the foundational laws of the program.

---

## 7. What Law 3 Organizes

Law 3 organizes a different but equally important thread:

- Project 4 narrow intervention gain
- Project 7 trigger-correctable vs trigger-insensitive family split
- Project 8 family-sensitive rescue architecture
- Project 9 family-sensitive rescue inside a higher-dimensional sandbox

Its role is to explain why:
- rescue should not be treated as universal
- and why architecture/intervention design must respect family-level heterogeneity

This makes Law 3 one of the strongest design-oriented laws in the program.

---

## 8. Why the Combination Is Powerful

Together, Law 1 and Law 3 imply a stronger general picture:

- local competence is insufficient
- rescue is non-universal
- therefore robust composition depends on matching the right support mechanism to the right failure structure

This is not a small statement.
It is one of the first genuine theory-level compressions of the full research line.

---

## 9. What This Does NOT Yet Mean

Project 10 does not yet imply:

- a full complete theory of arithmetic reasoning failure
- that the remaining candidate laws are already supported
- that no future result can challenge Law 1 or Law 3
- or that the program has reached final theoretical closure

This is still an early theory stage.

But it is no longer a purely exploratory one.

---

## 10. Most Important Next Step

The next step should be one of:

### A. test another candidate law
This would deepen the theory layer horizontally.

### B. test relations between laws
For example:
- is Law 3 partially downstream of Law 1?
- are they independent?
- do they define different layers of the same compositional phenomenon?

### C. build a first law map
A compact relation map among accepted and candidate laws

This may become the strongest next step.
