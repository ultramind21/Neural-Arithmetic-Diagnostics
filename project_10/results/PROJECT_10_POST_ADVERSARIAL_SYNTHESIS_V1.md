# PROJECT 10 POST-ADVERSARIAL SYNTHESIS V1
## What Survived, What Weakened, and What Changed

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** ACTIVE POST-ADVERSARIAL SYNTHESIS

---

## 1. Purpose

This document records the first post-adversarial synthesis of Project 10.

Project 10 began as a theory-building effort aimed at extracting and testing compositional failure laws from the broader research program.

It then entered an adversarial falsification phase.

This document records what survived that process, what weakened, and how the theory changed as a result.

---

## 2. What Survived Strongly

### Law 1
**Local competence is not sufficient for global robustness.**

This remains strongly supported.

Nothing in the adversarial phase challenged the central finding that local competence can be strong while global compositional robustness still fails.

This remains one of the foundational laws of Project 10.

---

## 3. What Survived, But With Explicit Bounds

### Law 3
**Rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal.**

This remains strongly supported within the original program evidence base.

However, the adversarial phase showed that this cannot be read without qualification.

The key new lesson is:

- family-aware rescue can outperform universal rescue under important baseline conditions
- but that advantage can shrink sharply under deep homogeneity
- and can reverse under sufficient universal mechanism amplification

So Law 3 survives,
but now as a bounded law rather than an unrestricted one.

---

## 4. What Did Not Survive in Its Original Form

### Original Higher-Order Candidate
The original stronger higher-order candidate did not survive unchanged.

Its necessity-style reading was too strong.

The adversarial phase showed that:
- family-aligned rescue is not generally required in all tested regimes
- and mechanism power plus structural homogeneity can both pressure or overturn that requirement

This is one of the most important outcomes of Project 10.

---

## 5. What Replaced It

A narrower higher-order revision is now more defensible:

> When local competence saturates without global robustness, family-aligned rescue tends to outperform universal rescue when family-level failure heterogeneity is substantive and universal mechanism power is not sufficient to override that heterogeneity.

This revised formulation is not yet an accepted law.

It is a revised higher-order hypothesis.

---

## 6. What the Adversarial Phase Taught

The adversarial phase taught at least four major lessons:

### A. Internal support is not enough
Component support and internal chain consistency do not guarantee a valid higher-order principle.

### B. Necessity claims are fragile
What first appears to be "required" may later turn out to be only comparatively advantageous under certain regimes.

### C. Boundary discovery is a scientific success
A theory that becomes narrower but more honest is stronger science than a broad but unstable claim.

### D. Mechanism power matters
Family-sensitive rescue is not the only variable.
Rescue power itself can change the comparative outcome.

---

## 7. Current Theory Structure

The current post-adversarial theory structure is:

### Foundational law
- Law 1: local competence is not sufficient for global robustness

### Strong but bounded law
- Law 3: family-sensitive rescue can outperform universal rescue under important baseline conditions

### Revised higher-order hypothesis
- family-aligned rescue advantage depends on substantive heterogeneity and mechanism-power limits

This is the most defensible current structure.

---

## 8. Why This Matters

This matters because Project 10 has now done something real:

- it extracted candidate laws
- it stress-tested them
- it weakened one when needed
- and it revised theory rather than protecting it

That is the difference between decorative theory and scientific theory-building.

---

## 9. Current Status

**Law 1:** strongly supported  
**Law 3:** strongly supported, explicitly bounded  
**Original higher-order candidate:** weakened / no longer acceptable in its stronger form  
**Revised higher-order candidate:** active hypothesis only  

Project 10 is therefore no longer in its original theory position.
It has entered a revised post-adversarial phase.
