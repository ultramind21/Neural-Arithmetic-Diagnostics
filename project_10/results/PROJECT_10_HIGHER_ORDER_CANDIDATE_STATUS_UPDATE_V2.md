---

## 2. Current Law Status

### Law 1
**Strongly supported**

Nothing in Regimes A, B, or C weakens the core finding that local competence is not sufficient for global robustness.

---

### Law 3
**Strongly supported, but now explicitly bounded**

Law 3 remains strongly supported within the current post-audit program evidence base.

However, Regimes B and C show that:
- family-sensitive rescue is not equally necessary under all structural conditions
- and not equally dominant under all mechanism-power regimes

This means Law 3 now requires explicit scope conditions.

---

## 3. Current Higher-Order Candidate Status

The higher-order candidate is now:

**Substantially weakened**

This is the correct current status.

It is too strong in its present form if read as saying that successful rescue requires family-aligned mechanisms in a broadly necessary sense.

The adversarial regimes have shown important counter-pressure against that reading.

---

## 4. What Regime A V2 Showed

Regime A V2 showed that:
- under aligned deep failure structure
- family-aware rescue still outperformed universal rescue
- but only with significant boundary pressure

This supported the higher-order candidate, but not cleanly enough for unrestricted claims.

---

## 5. What Regime B V1 Showed

Regime B V1 showed that:
- when families become deeply homogeneous in their failure mechanism
- universal rescue becomes nearly equivalent to family-aware rescue

This places strong pressure on any claim that family-aligned rescue is broadly necessary.

---

## 6. What Regime C V1 Showed

Regime C V1 showed that:
- when universal rescue power is amplified substantially
- universal rescue can outperform family-aware rescue even under strong family heterogeneity

This is a major challenge to the stronger version of the higher-order candidate.

It suggests that mechanism power can override family-sensitive advantage.

---

## 7. Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation now is:

> the higher-order candidate does not currently survive in its stronger necessity form; it must either be revised into a narrower conditional statement or abandoned in favor of a more precise theory.

This is the correct current position.

---

## 8. What Still Survives

The following still survive strongly:

1. local competence is not sufficient for global robustness
2. family-sensitive rescue can outperform universal rescue under important baseline regimes

What no longer survives cleanly is the stronger claim that:
- family-aligned rescue is generally required as a higher-order principle
