## 1. Purpose

This document records the final synthesis of Project 6.

Project 6 was launched to investigate a central mechanistic question:

> Where and how is arithmetic-relevant information represented inside the model, and how far can that internal structure explain behavior?

The project has now accumulated enough accepted evidence to support a strong final mechanistic position.

---

## 2. The Full Project 6 Arc

Project 6 developed through a coherent sequence of interpretability probes:

1. **Carry Selectivity**  
   carry-related information is measurably represented

2. **Success vs Failure Contrast**  
   successful and failing local arithmetic cases are internally distinguishable

3. **Top Unit Ablation**  
   some units are causally important for local digit-related behavior

4. **Family-Level Link**  
   internal structure varies across arithmetic family conditions

5. **Digit-vs-Carry Dissociation**  
   top digit-relevant and carry-relevant units are strongly dissociated

6. **Family-Level Causal Linkage Boundary**  
   local unit groups are not sufficient for a simple direct explanation of family-level behavior

7. **Sequence-Level Trajectory Differences**  
   rescued and failing families differ sharply in hidden-state trajectory structure

8. **Trajectory Intervention Boundary**  
   reducing oscillation alone does not rescue failing families

9. **Subspace-Level Probe**  
   arithmetic-relevant structure is visible as meaningful low-dimensional directions

10. **Subspace-Level Causal Intervention**  
    removal of the carry-related direction causes major local arithmetic degradation

Taken together, these probes form a strong and unusually complete mechanistic arc.

## 3. What Project 6 Has Established

Project 6 now establishes the following:

### A. Arithmetic-relevant internal structure is real
Carry-related information is clearly and measurably represented in hidden activations.

### B. Internal structure is behaviorally meaningful
Successful and failing local arithmetic cases are strongly separable in hidden activation space.

### C. Some units matter causally
Ablating top diagnostic units produces strong degradation in local digit/exact behavior.

### D. Digit and carry are internally specialized
Top digit-relevant and carry-relevant units can be strongly dissociated.

### E. Family-level behavior is linked to internal structure
The same internal structures vary meaningfully across arithmetic family types.

### F. Sequence-level internal dynamics matter
Rescued and failing families exhibit sharply different trajectory dynamics.

### G. Not all plausible causal levers work
Naive and selective trajectory smoothing do not rescue failing families.

### H. Arithmetic structure is geometric as well as local
Carry-related hidden-space directions are strongly visible and causally important.

This is a substantial mechanistic result set.

## 4. Strongest Positive Mechanistic Results

The strongest positive results of Project 6 are:

### 1. Internal carry structure exists
Carry is not only behaviorally relevant; it is measurably represented internally.

### 2. Internal success/failure structure exists
The hidden space is organized in a way that tracks behavioral correctness.

### 3. Functional specialization exists

### 4. Subspace-level causal importance exists

These results show that the arithmetic sandbox is not merely interpretable at the level of anecdotal activations. It supports real representational and causal analysis.

## 5. Strongest Boundary Results

Project 6 is strong not only because of what it found, but because of what it ruled out.

### A. Family-level behavior is not explained by simple local unit ablation alone
This means local signals are real, but not sufficient for full family-level explanation.

### B. Trajectory instability is not rescued by simple smoothing
This means the problem is more structured than a naive "stabilize the dynamics" story.

### C. The mechanistic picture is real but not simplistic
Project 6 rules out the temptation to jump from:
- "we found meaningful units"
to
- "therefore the full family-level mechanism is solved"

This is a major epistemic strength.

This is the strongest overall statement supported by the project.

Project 6 supports a real mechanistic story with both positive and negative causal structure.
