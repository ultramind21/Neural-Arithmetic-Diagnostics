# PROJECT 6 SUBSPACE RESULTS
## Low-Dimensional Geometric Structure Probe

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 6  
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

## Purpose

This document records the first accepted subspace-level interpretability result in Project 6.

Its purpose was to test whether arithmetic-relevant structure is visible not only in individual units, but also in low-dimensional internal directions.

---

## Main Result

The subspace probe showed that:

- carry-related structure is strongly visible as a direction in hidden space
- success-vs-failure structure is also visible as a direction
- these two directions are strongly related geometrically rather than being independent

This is the strongest geometric representation result in Project 6 so far.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. arithmetic-relevant structure is visible at the subspace level, not only at the single-unit level
2. carry-related distinction is especially strong geometrically
3. success-vs-failure distinction is also meaningfully represented as a direction
4. carry and success/failure directions are not independent; they are strongly coupled

This moves Project 6 into a more mature representational regime.

---

## Why This Matters

Earlier Project 6 results showed:
- carry-selective units
- success-vs-failure separation
- unit-level causal relevance
- digit-vs-carry dissociation
- and sequence-level differences

This result adds a new level:

> the arithmetic-relevant distinctions also organize the hidden space geometrically.

This is an important refinement because:
- unit-level views can be too narrow
- and family-level behavior may depend on low-dimensional structure that no single unit captures alone

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> carry and behavioral correctness are both strongly encoded in hidden space as low-dimensional directions, and those directions show substantial geometric coupling rather than complete independence.

This is a strong interpretability result.

---

## What This Does NOT Yet Prove

This result does not yet prove:

- a complete geometric theory of arithmetic computation
- that these directions alone are sufficient to control all behavior
- that the full family-level structure is solved by a low-dimensional account
- or that unit-level and subspace-level explanations are fully interchangeable

Those remain open.

---

## Why This Is Stronger Than Earlier Results

This result is stronger than earlier probes because it shows that the arithmetic-relevant structure:
- is not merely localized to a few interesting units
- and is not merely reflected in case-wise contrast
- but is also expressed as a meaningful geometric organization of hidden space

This is a major step toward richer mechanistic understanding.

---

## Scientific Value

Project 6 can now say that:
- arithmetic-relevant internal structure exists
- some units are causally important
- and the hidden space itself has meaningful arithmetic geometry

That combination gives the project a much stronger mechanistic identity.

---

## Recommended Next Step

A natural next direction is now:

- direct subspace intervention
- projection or removal of carry-related direction
- projection or removal of success/failure direction
- or causal comparison of unit-level vs subspace-level perturbation

This would move Project 6 from subspace observation toward subspace causality.
