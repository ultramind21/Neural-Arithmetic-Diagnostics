## 1. Purpose

This document records the refined synthesis point of Project 6 after the trajectory intervention result.

Project 6 began with the broad internal question:

> Where and how is arithmetic-relevant information represented inside the model?

It has now progressed to a stronger and more structured position:

> which internal structures are representationally visible, behaviorally meaningful, causally relevant, and where does their explanatory power stop?

This synthesis reflects that sharper state of the project.

---

## 2. Current Accepted Arc

Project 6 now contains eight accepted result layers:

1. **Carry Selectivity**  
   carry-related information is measurably represented

2. **Success vs Failure Contrast**  
   successful and failing local arithmetic cases are internally separable

3. **Top Unit Ablation**  
   some of the identified units are causally important for local digit-related behavior

4. **Family-Level Link**  
   the same internal structures vary across higher-level arithmetic family conditions

5. **Digit-vs-Carry Dissociation**  
   top digit-relevant and carry-relevant units can be sharply dissociated

6. **Family-Level Causal Linkage Boundary**  
   the identified local unit groups do not by themselves provide a simple direct explanation of family-level behavior

7. **Sequence-Level Trajectory Difference**  
   rescued and failing families differ strongly in trajectory stability and transition structure

8. **Trajectory Intervention Boundary**  
   reducing oscillation alone does not rescue failing family behavior and may damage successful family behavior

This is now a full mechanistic arc, not a loose collection of probes.

---

## 3. What Project 6 Has Established

Taken together, Project 6 now establishes the following:

### A. Arithmetic-relevant internal structure is real
Carry-sensitive internal signals are measurable and reproducible.

### B. Internal structure is behaviorally meaningful
Successful and failing local cases are distinguishable in hidden activation space.

### C. Some units matter causally for local behavior
The project has gone beyond correlation:
ablation causes clear asymmetric degradation in local digit/exact performance.

### D. Internal specialization exists
Digit-related and carry-relevant top units can be strongly separated.

### E. Family-level behavior is connected to internal structure
The same internal machinery varies systematically across arithmetic family types.

### F. But the explanation is not simple
Neither local unit ablation alone nor naive trajectory damping alone is sufficient to explain or repair family-level behavior completely.

This final point is crucial.

---

## 4. Strongest Positive Result

The strongest positive Project 6 result is:

> arithmetic-relevant internal structure is not only present, but partially specialized, behavior-linked, and causally important.

This is a substantial mechanistic finding.

The project can now say with confidence that:
- carry information is represented internally
- some units are especially informative
- some of them are functionally important for digit behavior
- and these internal signals connect meaningfully to broader family-level behavior

That is a real success.

---

## 5. Strongest Boundary Result

The strongest boundary result of Project 6 is:

> the current internal findings do not collapse into a simple direct family-level mechanism.

This boundary now has two forms:

### Unit-level boundary
Digit-relevant and carry-relevant local unit groups do not by themselves provide a simple direct explanation of family-level success and failure.

### Trajectory-level boundary
Reducing oscillatory trajectory instability alone does not rescue failing family behavior, and can damage successful family behavior.

This means the internal arithmetic story is real, but it is more structured than:
- "find a few units"
or
- "smooth the trajectory"

---

## 6. Updated Mechanistic Position

The refined mechanistic position of Project 6 is now:

> internal arithmetic structure exists, is behaviorally meaningful, and is partially causally important, but the family-level computational story depends on richer structured dynamics than simple local unit identity or naive stability interventions alone.

This is stronger than the earlier synthesis because it now includes a causal boundary on trajectory-level simplification.

---

## 7. Why This Matters Scientifically

This project matters because it demonstrates something difficult and valuable:

- mechanistic interpretability can succeed in this arithmetic sandbox
- but successful interpretability does not mean premature oversimplification

Project 6 does not stop at:
- "we found carry units"

It also shows:
- what those units explain
- what they do not explain
- and why apparently plausible simplifications fail

That combination makes the interpretability story much more credible.

- a proof that the currently identified structures are sufficient for total arithmetic understanding
- closure of the mechanistic interpretability problem

These remain open.

The strongest current Project 6 position is:

> the arithmetic sandbox now supports a real mechanistic interpretability program in which internal arithmetic structure is measurable, behaviorally linked, partially causally important, internally specialized, and family-sensitive—while also revealing that family-level computation cannot yet be reduced to simple local unit ablation or naive trajectory stabilization.

This is the strongest scientifically safe synthesis of the project.

## 11. Best Current Conceptual Lesson

The deepest lesson from Project 6 so far is:

> interpretability succeeds not when it finds a single neat explanation, but when it can identify both meaningful internal structure and the limits of overly simple explanations.

That is exactly what happened here.
