# PROJECT 6 FAMILY-LEVEL CAUSAL LINKAGE RESULTS
## Cross-Level Causal Probe

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 6  
**Status:** COMPLETE  
**Verdict:** PASS WITH QUALIFICATIONS

---

## Purpose

This document records the family-level causal linkage probe in Project 6.

Its purpose was to test whether the local unit groups identified earlier—especially digit-relevant and carry-relevant units—would directly explain family-level arithmetic behavior under ablation.

---

## Main Result

The result showed that family-level behavior is **not** trivially explained by direct ablation of the current top local unit groups.

In particular:

- some family outcomes remained unchanged under digit-unit ablation
- some family outcomes remained unchanged under carry-unit ablation
- and the local dissociation identified earlier did not automatically translate into a simple family-level causal decomposition

This is a strong and informative negative result.

---

## Why This Matters

Earlier Project 6 probes established:

- carry-selective internal structure
- success-vs-failure internal contrast
- causal importance of some units for local digit behavior
- family-level variation in those internal units
- and digit-vs-carry dissociation

A natural next hypothesis was that these unit groups might directly explain family-level successes and failures.

This probe shows that the story is more complex.

That is scientifically valuable because it prevents premature reduction of the family-level phenomenon to a simple unit-level account.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. local mechanistic signals are real
2. local causal unit importance is real
3. but family-level arithmetic behavior is not trivially reducible to direct ablation of the currently identified top local units
4. therefore a stronger cross-level explanation is still needed

This is an important boundary result.

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> the currently identified digit-relevant and carry-relevant local units are mechanistically meaningful, but they do not by themselves provide a simple direct explanation of family-level success and failure under the current ablation test.

This means the internal story is real, but not yet complete.

---

## Why This Is Still a Positive Result

This result is valuable because it rules out a tempting oversimplification.

Without this probe, one might wrongly conclude:
- once local digit-vs-carry specialization is found, family-level behavior is already explained

This probe shows that such a conclusion would be premature.

That is precisely the kind of correction good mechanistic work should produce.

---

## What This Does NOT Yet Prove

This result does not prove:

- that the local units are unimportant
- that the family-level split is unrelated to internal structure
- that no mechanistic explanation exists
- or that local-to-global linkage cannot be recovered with better probes

It proves only that:
> the currently tested direct ablation path is not sufficient for a simple family-level explanation.

---

## Scientific Position After This Result

Project 6 now supports a more refined position:

- internal arithmetic structure exists
- some units matter causally for local behavior
- local digit-vs-carry specialization is visible
- but family-level arithmetic success and failure are not explained in a simple one-step way by direct ablation of these local unit groups

This is a stronger and more mature interpretability position than either:
- "we found the units, so we solved it"
or
- "the probes failed"

---

## Recommended Next Step

A stronger future direction would be one of:

- multi-unit structured ablation beyond the current top-k sets
- probing sequence-level intermediate states rather than only local hidden states
- tracing error propagation across positions
- examining whether family-level effects arise from repeated small local distortions rather than direct unit failure alone
