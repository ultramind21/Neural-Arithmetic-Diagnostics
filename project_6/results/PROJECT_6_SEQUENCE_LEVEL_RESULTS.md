# PROJECT 6 SEQUENCE-LEVEL RESULTS
## Internal Trajectory Stability Across Families

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 6  
**Status:** COMPLETE  
**Verdict:** PASS

---

## Purpose

This document records the first sequence-level interpretability result in Project 6.

Its purpose was to test whether the rescued and failing arithmetic families differ not only in local representation, but also in their internal state trajectories across positions.

---

## Main Result

The sequence-level probe showed a sharp contrast in internal trajectory dynamics across family types.

### Observed family patterns
- `alternating_carry` showed strong persistent oscillation
- `full_propagation_chain` showed rapid stabilization
- `block_boundary_stress` showed structured transitions with local stabilization between jumps

This is the strongest family-level mechanistic signal obtained so far in Project 6.

---

## Why This Matters

Earlier Project 6 probes established:
- representational carry signal
- success-vs-failure contrast
- causal importance of selected units
- family-level internal linkage
- digit-vs-carry specialization
- and a boundary on simple local-to-family causal reduction

This new result adds something qualitatively stronger:

> the rescued and failing families differ at the level of internal trajectory dynamics across the sequence.

This means the mechanistic story is no longer only about static hidden-state differences.
It is now also about how internal state evolves over time.

---

## Family-Level Interpretation

### Alternating Carry
The failing alternating family is associated with:
- large trajectory differences at nearly every step
- repeated oscillatory movement rather than convergence
- no stable internal settling

This is consistent with sequence-level instability.

### Full Propagation Chain
The rescued family is associated with:
- a small initial transition
- followed by near-complete stabilization

This is consistent with fast convergence to a stable internal state.

### Block-Boundary Stress
The successful family is associated with:
- a small number of strong but localized jumps
- stable plateaus between those jumps

This is consistent with controlled transition structure rather than uncontrolled oscillation.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. family-level arithmetic success and failure are associated with qualitatively different internal state trajectories
2. not all successful families rely on the same kind of internal dynamics
3. oscillatory internal dynamics are a strong candidate explanation for at least one major failure family
4. stabilized or controlled trajectory structure is associated with successful family behavior

This is a major mechanistic step.

---

## Why This Is Stronger Than Earlier Results

Earlier Project 6 results showed:
- that internal signals exist
- that some units are selective
- that some are causally important
- and that family-level behavior is not trivially reducible to simple local unit ablation

This result goes further by showing that:
- the family split is also reflected in temporal/internal trajectory form

That makes it one of the strongest integrative results of the project.

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> rescued and failing arithmetic families differ sharply in sequence-level internal trajectory stability, with failure associated with persistent oscillation and success associated with rapid stabilization or structured transitions.

This is a strong mechanistic signal, though not yet a complete final theory.

---

## What This Does NOT Yet Prove

This result does not yet prove:

- the exact dynamical mechanism generating the oscillation
- that oscillation is the only cause of family failure
- that all successful family behavior is fully explained by trajectory stabilization alone
- a complete sequence-level circuit decomposition

These remain open.

That gives Project 6 its strongest integrative mechanistic result so far.
