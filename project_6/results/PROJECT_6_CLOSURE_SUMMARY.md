## 1. Project 6 Mandate

Project 6 was launched as a new project after the Project 5 checkpoint.

Its purpose was not to optimize arithmetic performance, but to investigate a more internal question:

> Where and how is arithmetic-relevant information represented inside the model?

Project 6 therefore served as a mechanistic interpretability sandbox rather than a benchmark project.

---

## 2. What Project 6 Built

Project 6 established a clean interpretability arc through six probes:

1. carry selectivity
2. success-vs-failure internal contrast
3. top-unit ablation
4. family-level linkage
5. digit-vs-carry dissociation
6. family-level causal linkage boundary

This sequence moved the project from:
- representation detection
to
- behavioral contrast
to
- causal-style perturbation
to
- cross-level interpretive boundaries

---

## 3. Summary of Accepted Results

### Result 1 — Carry Selectivity
Project 6 showed that carry-related information is measurably represented in hidden activations, and that the carry-conditioned local model exhibits stronger carry selectivity than the simpler local baseline.

### Result 2 — Success vs Failure Contrast
Project 6 showed that successful and failing local arithmetic cases occupy distinguishable regions of hidden activation space.

### Result 3 — Top Unit Ablation
Project 6 showed that ablating top diagnostic units causes a substantial drop in local digit/exact performance while leaving carry performance intact.

### Result 4 — Family-Level Link
Project 6 showed that the same internal arithmetic structures vary across behaviorally distinct arithmetic families.

### Result 5 — Digit-vs-Carry Dissociation
Project 6 showed that top digit-relevant units and top carry-relevant units can be fully dissociated at the top-unit level.

### Result 6 — Family-Level Causal Linkage Boundary
Project 6 showed that these local mechanistic unit groups do not by themselves provide a simple direct explanation of family-level success and failure.

---

## 4. Main Scientific Position

The strongest final scientific position of Project 6 is:

> arithmetic-relevant internal structure exists, is behaviorally meaningful, and is partially causally important; however, the currently identified local unit structure is not yet sufficient for a simple direct explanation of higher-level family behavior.

This is the central closure position of the project.

---

## 5. Why Project 6 Matters

Project 6 matters because it demonstrates that arithmetic can serve not only as a behavioral benchmark, but also as a genuine mechanistic interpretability sandbox.

The project shows that one can:
- identify carry-sensitive internal structure
- connect internal state to local correctness
- perturb identified units causally
- and test the limits of local-to-global explanation

This is a substantial methodological result.

---

## 6. What Project 6 Established

Project 6 established the following:

- internal carry-sensitive signals exist
- local success and failure are internally distinguishable
- some units are causally important for digit-related local behavior
- digit and carry signals can be functionally specialized
- local mechanistic findings connect meaningfully to family-level structure
- but local unit-level explanation is not yet sufficient for full family-level causal closure

---

## 7. What Project 6 Does NOT Establish

Project 6 does not establish:

- a full arithmetic circuit
- a complete sequence-level mechanistic explanation
- a final explanation of all family-level differences
- or a total closure of the interpretability problem

These remain open for future work.
