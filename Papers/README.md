# PAPERS — Research Narratives and Project Closures
## Project 03 Abacus

This folder contains the research-facing narrative layer of the repository.

Its role is different from:
- `src/` → core executable code
- `final_audit/` → trust-recovery verification archive
- `project_4/` → post-audit diagnostic framework development

Instead, `papers/` preserves the conceptual and historical research record.

---

## What This Folder Represents

The repository contains a full research line, not a single isolated experiment.

Within that research line:

- **Projects 1–3** established the original sequence of empirical questions, findings, failures, and conceptual advances
- **The audit** later revisited those projects to determine what remained trustworthy, what required qualification, and what had to be bounded more carefully
- **Project 4** then began as a post-audit methodological development

This `papers/` folder preserves the narrative and closure documents associated especially with the earlier research line.

---

## Main Use of This Folder

Use `papers/` when you want to understand:

- how the original research line evolved
- what Projects 1–3 were trying to establish
- how the conceptual interpretations were originally framed
- what final closure-style statements were written for those projects
- how the killer-test and related findings were historically presented

Use `final_audit/` when you want the stricter verification-aligned position.

---

## Important Reading Principle

Some documents in `papers/` are historical narrative/closure texts written before or alongside the full trust-recovery audit.

Therefore:

- these files remain important and valuable
- but they should be read together with the audit-aligned documents in `final_audit/`
- if a paper-facing document and an audit document differ in strength of claim, the audit-aligned position takes precedence

This is a deliberate feature of the repository, not a contradiction:
it preserves both the research history and the later verification discipline.

## Relationship to the Audit

This folder is not the final authority on what is currently considered fully supported.

That role belongs to the audit archive.

Instead, `papers/` serves as:
- the research memory of the repository
- the narrative layer of Projects 1–3
- the place where the original claims, interpretations, and closures remain visible

This makes the repository intellectually honest:
it preserves not only the final corrected view, but also the path by which that view was reached.

## Final Note

Projects 1–3 are not obsolete leftovers.
