## Purpose

This document records the digit-vs-carry dissociation result in Project 6.

Its purpose was to test whether the units most relevant to digit-related local behavior overlap substantially with the units most relevant to carry-related internal distinction.

---

## Main Result

The result showed a complete separation between the top units identified for:

- digit-related success/failure distinction
and
- carry-related internal selectivity

### Observed result
- top digit-relevant units: [16, 18, 12, 1, 31]
- top carry-relevant units: [0, 19, 9, 15, 29]
- overlap count: **0**

This is the strongest internal specialization result obtained in Project 6 so far.

---

## Why This Matters

This result suggests that the model is not using a single undifferentiated hidden substrate for all arithmetic-relevant behavior.

Instead, it appears to organize at least part of its internal computation into:
- units that are more important for digit-related local success
- and units that are more important for carry-related internal distinction

This is strong evidence for internal functional specialization.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. digit-related and carry-related internal signals are not maximally entangled
2. the top units for digit behavior and carry representation can be completely dissociated under the current probe
3. Project 6 has now moved beyond generic hidden-signal analysis into structured internal specialization analysis

This is a major interpretability result.

---

## Why This Is Stronger Than Earlier Project 6 Results

Earlier Project 6 probes established:
- carry selectivity
- internal success-vs-failure contrast
- causal importance of top units
- family-level activation differences

This result adds something sharper:

> not only do internal arithmetic-relevant signals exist, but they also separate into distinct functional unit groups at the top-unit level.

That is a stronger form of structural evidence.

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> under the current probe, digit-relevant and carry-relevant internal units are fully dissociated at the top-unit level, providing strong evidence of internal functional specialization.

This is a strong result, but it does not yet imply a full solved arithmetic circuit.

---

## What This Does NOT Yet Prove

This result does not yet prove:

- that digit and carry are globally separated throughout the full network
- that there is no redundancy beyond the top units
- that the complete local arithmetic mechanism is fully decomposed
- that the same dissociation directly explains all higher-level family behavior

Those remain open questions.
