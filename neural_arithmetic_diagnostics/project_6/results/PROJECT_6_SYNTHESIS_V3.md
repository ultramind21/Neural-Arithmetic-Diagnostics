## 1. Purpose

This document records the most refined synthesis point reached so far in Project 6.

Project 6 began with a broad mechanistic question:

> Where and how is arithmetic-relevant information represented inside the model?

It has now progressed to a much sharper question:

> Which internal structures are behaviorally meaningful, causally relevant, and what simple mechanistic explanations can already be ruled out?

This synthesis reflects the strongest current answer to that question.

---

## 3. What Project 6 Has Positively Established

The strongest positive findings now support the following:

### A. Carry-related information is represented internally
Carry information is not hidden in an uninterpretable way. It leaves a measurable representational signal.

### B. Hidden states track behavioral correctness
Successful and failing local arithmetic cases are separable in hidden activation space.

### C. Some units matter causally for local digit behavior
Ablation of the top diagnostic units causes a substantial drop in local digit/exact performance while leaving carry performance intact.

### D. Internal specialization exists
Digit-relevant and carry-relevant top units can be sharply dissociated.

### E. Local internal findings connect to family-level structure
The identified internal structures vary meaningfully across behaviorally distinct arithmetic families.

These are already strong mechanistic results.

---

## 4. What Project 6 Has Ruled Out

Project 6 is now equally strong in what it has excluded.

### A. Family-level behavior is not explained by local unit identity alone
The currently identified local unit groups do not provide a simple direct family-level explanation under ablation.

### B. Family-level failure is not rescued by naive global trajectory stabilization
Uniform hidden-state damping reduces oscillation but does not restore failing-family behavior, and can damage successful-family behavior.

### C. Family-level failure is not rescued by selective large-jump suppression
Even a more targeted smoothing intervention fails to rescue the failing family and can still harm successful dynamics.

This is very important.
It means the project has moved beyond "we found interesting units" toward a much sharper exclusion map.

---

## 5. The Strongest Current Mechanistic Position

The strongest current Project 6 position is:

> arithmetic-relevant internal structure is real, behaviorally meaningful, and partially causally important, but higher-level family success and failure are not reducible to a simple local-unit story or a simple trajectory-stabilization story.

This is the most scientifically defensible statement at the current stage.

It preserves:
- the positive mechanistic findings
- and the newly established causal boundaries

without collapsing them into an oversimplified theory.

---

## 6. Why the Trajectory Results Matter So Much

The trajectory results are especially important because they close an attractive but overly simple mechanistic path.

Before them, one might reasonably think:
- failing families differ because their trajectories are unstable
- therefore making those trajectories smoother should rescue performance

Project 6 now shows that this is not enough.

The observed sequence-level dynamics are real and highly informative, but:
- oscillation is not itself the simple rescue handle
- and large-jump suppression is not the right intervention by itself

This makes the interpretability story more serious and more mature.

---

## 7. Why This Is a Strong Scientific Position

A common weakness in interpretability work is stopping too early:
- after finding a signal
- after identifying a few units
- or after seeing a compelling visualization

Project 6 did not stop there.

It kept asking:
- do these signals matter?
- do they explain behavior?
- do they explain family-level behavior?
- can they be manipulated causally?
- and do simple mechanistic interventions actually rescue the problem?

That sequence is what makes the current position strong.

---

## 8. What Project 6 Now Suggests

The project now suggests a much sharper view of the remaining open problem:

- local arithmetic-relevant units exist
- local specialization exists
- trajectory differences are real
- but family-level behavior depends on a richer composition of internal structures than any simple one-factor explanation tested so far

In short:

> the remaining mechanistic problem is not the absence of interpretable structure, but the insufficiency of currently isolated structure to explain the full family-level phenomenon.

That is a more advanced and more useful mechanistic frontier.

---

## 9. What Project 6 Does NOT Yet Establish

Project 6 still does not establish:

- a complete arithmetic circuit
- a full local-to-global causal decomposition
- a final explanation of all family-level behavior
- a proof that the currently identified units are sufficient for full arithmetic understanding
- a final mechanistic closure of the sandbox

These remain open.

But importantly:
- the project now knows much better what the open problem actually is.

The strongest current lesson from Project 6 is:

> mechanistic understanding progresses not only by finding interpretable signals, but also by ruling out attractive but insufficient explanations.

That is exactly what happened here.

Project 6 identified real internal arithmetic structure, then showed that neither local-unit explanation alone nor naive trajectory stabilization alone is sufficient for full family-level understanding.

This is a high-quality scientific position.
