- "one more probe"

- deeper causal structure

- and more ambitious bridges between local mechanism and family-level behavior

---

## 3. Frontier A — Trajectory-Preserving Intervention

### Question
Can family-level failure be improved by modifying trajectory dynamics without destroying useful internal structure?

### Why it matters
The first damping intervention showed that naive stabilization is not enough.
The next question is whether more selective intervention can preserve useful dynamics while suppressing harmful instability.

---

## 4. Frontier B — Subspace-Level Causal Intervention

### Question
Are the important arithmetic signals better understood at the level of subspaces or directions rather than individual units?

### Why it matters
Unit-level analysis may be too coarse.
A subspace-level intervention could reveal a more faithful internal decomposition of arithmetic-relevant structure.

---

## 5. Frontier C — Family-Conditioned Trajectory Geometry

### Question
Do different arithmetic families induce different attractor-like or oscillatory state geometries?

### Why it matters
This would move the project from simple trajectory statistics toward a richer geometric view of family-level internal dynamics.

---

## 6. Frontier D — Counterfactual Trajectory Editing

### Question
If trajectory segments from successful families are inserted or imitated in failing families, does behavior change?

### Why it matters
This could provide much stronger causal evidence connecting trajectory structure to family-level success.

---

## 7. Frontier E — Bridge Back to Project 5

### Question
Why does local mechanistic competence fail to scale into successful composition?

### Why it matters
This is one of the deepest open questions across the whole research line.
A strong answer here could connect decomposition, robustness, and interpretability into one unified explanation.

---

## 8. Strategic Recommendation

Project 6 should continue through one of two best next directions:

### More immediately tractable
- **Trajectory-preserving intervention**
- **Family-conditioned trajectory geometry**

### More ambitious
- **Subspace-level causal intervention**
- **Counterfactual trajectory editing**

---

## 9. Guiding Rule

The project should not continue by random probe accumulation.

Each next step should:
- answer a sharper question than the previous one
- add explanatory depth
- and increase causal precision

That is how Project 6 reaches the next scientific level.
