# PROJECT 6 FAMILY-LEVEL LINK RESULTS
## Family-Sensitive Activation Modulation in Diagnostic Units

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 6  
**Status:** COMPLETE  
**Verdict:** PASS

---

## Purpose

This document records the first accepted family-level interpretability link in Project 6.

Its purpose was to test whether the units identified earlier through local success/failure probes also reflect higher-level family structure differences.

---

## Main Result

The result showed that the same small set of diagnostic units identified locally also exhibits systematic activation differences across arithmetic families.

This is the strongest Project 6 result so far because it links three levels of evidence:

1. local carry-related representation
2. local success-vs-failure distinction
3. family-level behavioral structure

---

## What Was Observed

The key diagnostic units did not maintain a single fixed activation profile across all families.

Instead, they showed:
- family-dependent modulation
- large magnitude changes
- and in some cases sign reversals across families

This means the units are not merely globally "important" in an undifferentiated sense.
They appear to participate differently depending on the structural family context.

---

## Why This Matters

This result is important because it provides the first strong bridge between:

- local mechanistic signals
and
- higher-level family behavior

Earlier Project 6 results showed:
- carry selectivity
- internal contrast between successful and failing cases
- causal-style importance of top units under ablation

This result extends that chain by showing that:
- the same units are also sensitive to family-level structural differences

This makes the interpretability story much more coherent.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. the top diagnostic units are not only local-task relevant
2. they also track larger structural differences across arithmetic families
3. family-level success and failure are not disconnected from the internal signals already identified locally
4. Project 6 now has a meaningful cross-level interpretability link

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> a small set of units identified through local diagnostic probes also exhibits family-sensitive activation modulation, suggesting that local internal arithmetic structure and family-level behavior are linked rather than independent.

This is a strong interpretability result.

---

## What This Does NOT Yet Prove

This result does not yet prove:

- a full arithmetic circuit
- a complete causal mechanism for family-level success or failure
- that the observed units alone are sufficient to explain all family-level behavior
- that all important internal structure has already been identified

Those remain open.

---

## Why This Is the Strongest Project 6 Result So Far

This result is especially strong because it unifies earlier probes:

- Probe 1: carry selectivity exists
- Probe 2: success and failure are internally distinguishable
- Probe 3: top diagnostic units matter under ablation
- Probe 4: those same units also reflect family-level structural differences

Together, these results make Project 6 much more than a set of isolated exploratory probes.

They form an emerging mechanistic picture.

---

## Scientific Value

Project 6 can now claim something significantly stronger than at the start:

- internal arithmetic-related structure is measurable
- some of that structure is behaviorally important
- and some of the same units are modulated systematically by family-level structure

That is a meaningful mechanistic interpretability contribution, even if it is not yet a complete mechanistic theory.
