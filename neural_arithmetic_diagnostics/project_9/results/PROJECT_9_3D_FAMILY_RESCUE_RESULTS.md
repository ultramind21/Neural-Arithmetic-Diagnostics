## Purpose

This document records the first rescue experiment result in the higher-dimensional compositional sandbox.

Its purpose was to test whether rescue logic itself behaves differently across family types.

---

## Main Result

The experiment showed that rescue behavior is itself family-sensitive.

In particular:

- same-family rescue did not improve the perturbation outcome
- cross-family rescue could make the perturbation spread substantially worse
- one family remained unaffected by either rescue strategy

This is a strong result.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. family-sensitive structure in the 3D world is not limited to failure patterns alone
2. rescue behavior is also family-sensitive
3. cross-family intervention can amplify disruption rather than repair it
4. same-family intervention can be safer but still insufficient

This makes the 3D sandbox considerably richer than a simple propagation toy.

---

This result matters because it pushes Project 9 beyond:
- local-to-global propagation

- and family-sensitive influence

It now shows that:
- even attempted repair or rescue must be treated as family-structured

That is a major conceptual advance.

## Family-Level Interpretation

### Family 0 and Family 2
Cross-family rescue increased disruption substantially, indicating that intervention from outside the local family type can be harmful.

### Family 1
The same rescue logic did not change the outcome, suggesting that this family may obey a different structural regime.

This is exactly the kind of family-sensitive difference Project 9 was meant to uncover.
