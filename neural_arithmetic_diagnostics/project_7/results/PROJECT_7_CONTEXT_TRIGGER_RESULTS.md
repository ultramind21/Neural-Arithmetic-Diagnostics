## Purpose

This document records the first surgical context-trigger intervention result in Project 7.

Its purpose was to test whether the recurring local trigger context identified earlier is causally sufficient to explain family-level failure.

---

## Main Result

The same trigger correction produced sharply different outcomes across families.

### Observed outcome
- `block_boundary_stress` was rescued completely
- `alternating_carry` remained failed
- `full_propagation_chain` remained successful and unaffected

This is a major causal result.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. family-level failures are not mechanistically uniform
2. at least one family is strongly trigger-driven
3. at least one other family is not rescued by the same local correction
4. therefore the family-level failure landscape contains more than one causal mode

This is one of the strongest results in Project 7 so far.

---

## Why This Matters

Before this intervention, it was still plausible that:
- all major family-level failures might be explained by the same recurring local trigger structure

This result shows that this is not the case.

The project can now distinguish at least two kinds of family-level failure:
- a trigger-correctable failure mode
- a trigger-insensitive failure mode

This is a major advance in the local-to-global explanation problem.

---

## Family-Level Interpretation

### Block-Boundary Stress
This family appears to be strongly driven by the identified recurring local trigger context.

Correcting that context was sufficient to rescue global exact-match behavior.

### Alternating Carry
This family is not rescued by the same trigger intervention.

This means its failure depends on something more than the currently identified local trigger.

### Full Propagation Chain
This family does not depend on the trigger and remains successful without intervention.

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> different family-level failures arise from different local-to-global mechanisms; some are trigger-correctable and others are not.

This is a strong causal finding.

---

## What This Does NOT Yet Prove

This result does not yet prove:

- the full mechanism of alternating failure
- the complete taxonomy of all family-level failure modes
- that the identified trigger is the only important trigger in the rescued family
- or that Project 7 has fully solved the local-to-global bridge problem

But it does prove that the family-level problem is not mechanistically uniform.

---

## Scientific Value

This result is especially valuable because it moves the project beyond:
- "there are internal differences"
toward:
- "different family failures may require different mechanistic explanations"

That is exactly the kind of result that makes Project 7 scientifically deep rather than merely descriptive.

## Recommended Next Step

The next natural question is:

That is now the strongest next mechanistic frontier.
