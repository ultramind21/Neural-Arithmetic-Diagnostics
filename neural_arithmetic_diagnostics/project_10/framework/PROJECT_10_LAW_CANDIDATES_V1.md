## Purpose

This document defines the first explicit candidate laws for Project 10.

These are not yet accepted laws.
They are candidate statements extracted from Projects 4–9 and will only become stronger if they survive targeted falsification attempts.

---

## Candidate Law 1
### Name
**Local Competence Is Not Sufficient for Global Robustness**

### Condition
A model shows strong local arithmetic competence.

### Outcome
This does not guarantee robust global compositional success.

### Boundary
This may fail if an architecture provides explicit successful local-to-global support mechanisms.

### Why it is a candidate
This is one of the strongest repeated findings across Projects 5–8.

### What would falsify it?
A system with strong local competence but no further special support that nevertheless achieves broad robust composition across diverse families.

---

## Candidate Law 2
### Name
**Family-Level Failure Is Mechanistically Heterogeneous**

### Condition
A model fails on multiple family-level structured cases.

### Outcome
Those failures may arise from different mechanisms rather than from one shared breakdown.

### Boundary
This could fail if a future system shows that all family failures collapse under a single intervention.

### Why it is a candidate
Project 7 strongly supports this as a current organizing principle.

### What would falsify it?
A single intervention that rescues all major family-level failure types under conditions previously thought heterogeneous.

---

## Candidate Law 3
### Name
**Rescue Mechanisms Are Family-Sensitive Rather Than Universal**

### Condition
A rescue mechanism is introduced into a compositional system.

### Outcome
Its benefit may depend strongly on family type rather than generalize uniformly.

### Boundary
This may fail if a future architecture demonstrates one robust rescue mechanism that works broadly across all tested families.

### Why it is a candidate
Projects 4, 7, and 8 all support family-sensitive rescue behavior.

### What would falsify it?
A single architecture component or rescue policy that shows broad consistent success across all major family types without family-sensitive adjustment.

---

## Candidate Law 4
### Name
**Internal Arithmetic Structure Can Be Real Without Being Globally Sufficient**

### Condition
A model contains meaningful carry-sensitive, behavior-linked, or causally relevant internal arithmetic structure.

### Outcome
This still does not guarantee complete family-level or global compositional robustness.

### Boundary
This may fail if future work shows that the identified internal structures, without further additions, are sufficient for broad global success.

### Why it is a candidate
Project 6 strongly supports this.

### What would falsify it?
A direct demonstration that the already identified local internal structures fully explain and rescue the larger family-level behavior.

---

## Candidate Law 5
### Name
**Topology and Family Structure Jointly Shape Propagation in Higher-Dimensional Worlds**

### Condition
Composition is embedded in a higher-dimensional structured state space.

### Outcome
Propagation and rescue behavior depend jointly on topology and family identity.

### Boundary
This may fail if larger or alternative higher-dimensional sandboxes show only trivial or uniform behavior.

### Why it is a candidate
Project 9 introduced the first evidence for this.

### What would falsify it?
A higher-dimensional sandbox where topology-sensitive and family-sensitive differentiation disappears under controlled scale-up.

---

## Candidate Law 6
### Name
**Naive Stabilization Is Not Necessarily the Correct Causal Lever**

### Condition
A family-level failure is associated with unstable or oscillatory internal dynamics.

### Outcome
Simply stabilizing or smoothing those dynamics may not restore correct behavior, and may even damage successful behavior.

### Boundary
This may fail if a stronger stabilization intervention later demonstrates broad rescue without collateral damage.

### Why it is a candidate
Project 6 produced strong negative evidence here.

### What would falsify it?
A direct stabilization intervention that rescues failing families while preserving successful ones.
