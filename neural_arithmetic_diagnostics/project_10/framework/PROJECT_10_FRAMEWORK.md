# PROJECT 10 FRAMEWORK
## Compositional Failure Laws

**Date:** April 2026  
**Status:** ACTIVE FRAMEWORK  
**Relation to previous work:** New theory-building project after Projects 4–9

---

## 1. Core Question

Project 10 asks:

> What general laws govern when local competence, internal structure, and targeted rescue still fail—or succeed—to produce globally robust compositional behavior?

This is the central question of the project.

---

## 2. Why This Project Exists

Projects 4–9 established a large body of evidence about:
- narrow gains
- structured failures
- decomposition bottlenecks
- mechanistic signals
- heterogeneous family behavior
- architecture-level rescue
- and higher-dimensional compositional structure

The program is now rich enough that continued progress should not depend only on more experiments.

It should now ask whether these results can be compressed into:
- laws
- invariants
- boundaries
- and falsifiable principles

Project 10 exists to make that move.

---

## 3. What Counts as a Law

A statement counts as a law candidate in Project 10 only if it has all four of:

### A. Condition
When does it apply?

### B. Outcome
What does it predict?

### C. Boundary
Where does it stop applying?

### D. Testability
How could it be falsified?

Without these four, the statement is not yet a law.
It is only:
- a pattern
- a hypothesis
- or an intuition

---

## 4. Core Methodological Principle

Project 10 does not begin by trying to prove laws.

It begins by trying to **break them**.

This means:
- candidate laws must face targeted falsification tests
- laws that survive become stronger
- laws that fail are revised, narrowed, or discarded

This is the main discipline of the project.

---

## 5. Main Layers of Project 10

### Layer A — Candidate Law Extraction
Extract law candidates from Projects 4–9

### Layer B — Law Stress Testing
Design targeted experiments to falsify those candidates

### Layer C — Boundary Mapping
Identify where each candidate holds and where it breaks

### Layer D — Minimal Theory
Assemble only the candidates that survive into a coherent compact theory

---

## 6. Initial Candidate Themes

Project 10 will begin with candidate law themes such as:

- local competence is not sufficient for global robustness
- family-level failures are mechanistically heterogeneous
- rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal
- internal structure can be causally real without being globally sufficient
- topology and family structure jointly shape propagation in higher-dimensional worlds
- naive stabilization is not necessarily the correct causal lever

These are starting points, not final laws.

---

## 7. Non-Goals

Project 10 does not aim to:
- create a decorative pseudo-theory
- rename observations as "laws" without testing
- continue random experimentation without abstraction
- or close the entire research line with premature generality

It aims to produce compact, testable, bounded general statements.
