# PROJECT 9 SMALL 3D LATTICE V2 RESULTS

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 9  
**Status:** COMPLETE

## Purpose

This document records the second higher-dimensional sandbox result in Project 9.

Its purpose was to test whether scaling the first sandbox from `2×2×2` to `3×2×2` would reveal topology-sensitive perturbation differences.

---

## Main Result

The larger lattice broke the symmetry observed in the first sandbox.

In `2×2×2`, all positions had the same perturbation reach count.

In `3×2×2`, this changed:
- `edge_like` positions produced a larger perturbation reach
- `corner_like` positions produced a smaller perturbation reach

This is the first clear topology-sensitive differentiation result in Project 9.

---

## What This Establishes

This result establishes the following:

1. the higher-dimensional sandbox becomes structurally richer when slightly scaled
2. perturbation influence is no longer uniform across positions
3. positional role inside the lattice matters
4. the sandbox now supports meaningful topology-sensitive analysis, not only generic local-to-global propagation

This is a major advance beyond the first sandbox result.

---

## Why This Matters

This matters because it shows that the 3D sandbox is not merely a symmetric toy world.

The first sandbox established:
- local-to-global effects exist

The second sandbox now shows:
- not all positions matter equally

That means the sandbox is beginning to develop the kind of structural heterogeneity that makes it scientifically useful for deeper compositional reasoning studies.

---

## Edge vs Corner Difference

The clearest current distinction is:

- `edge_like` positions have stronger perturbation reach
- `corner_like` positions have weaker perturbation reach

This creates the first meaningful topology-based role distinction in Project 9.

That is a strong sign that further scale-up or richer local rules may produce even more informative structure.

---

## Carry-Dynamic Note

The result also preserves a second important observation:

- perturbation reach count and carry-dynamic response are not the same thing

Even where changed-count structure becomes clearer, carry-count changes remain non-uniform and position-sensitive.

This means the sandbox is becoming richer at both:
- the topology level
- and the arithmetic-dynamic level

---

## Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> scaling the lattice from 2×2×2 to 3×2×2 is sufficient to reveal topology-sensitive influence classes, with edge-like and corner-like positions already behaving differently under perturbation.

This is a strong Project 9 result.

> the sandbox has moved from symmetric local-to-global propagation into topology-sensitive behavior.
