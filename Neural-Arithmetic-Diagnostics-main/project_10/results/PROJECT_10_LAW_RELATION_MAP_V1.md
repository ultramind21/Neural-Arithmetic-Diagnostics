## 1. Purpose

This document defines the first structural relation map among accepted and candidate laws in Project 10.

The purpose is not only to list laws, but to ask:

> How do the current laws relate to one another?

This is necessary for moving from:
- multiple isolated law statements
to
- a more coherent theory structure

---

## 2. Current Strongly Supported Laws

### Law 1
**Local competence is not sufficient for global robustness.**

### Law 3
**Rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal.**

These are currently the strongest accepted law candidates in the program.

---

## 3. The Main Relation Question

The central question is:

> Is Law 3 a consequence of Law 1, an extension of it, or a partially independent law?

This is the first relation question Project 10 should address.

---

## 4. Candidate Relation Structure

### Interpretation A — Law 3 as a downstream consequence of Law 1
Under this reading:

- if local competence is not sufficient for global robustness,
- then rescue mechanisms may need to address family-specific global failure structure,
- which would naturally make rescue family-sensitive rather than universal.

In this view:
- Law 1 is deeper
- Law 3 is a structural consequence of it

---

### Interpretation B — Law 1 and Law 3 as partially independent
Under this reading:

- Law 1 concerns the gap between local competence and global robustness
- Law 3 concerns the non-universality of rescue behavior

These two may overlap strongly, but neither fully reduces to the other.

In this view:
- both laws are major
- but they capture different aspects of the compositional problem

---

### Interpretation C — Law 3 as a refinement layer on top of Law 1
Under this reading:

- Law 1 defines the existence of the local-to-global gap
- Law 3 refines what kinds of rescue logic are needed once that gap exists

This makes Law 1 the more foundational law,
while Law 3 operates at the intervention/architecture layer.

This is currently the strongest working interpretation.

---

## 5. Current Best Working Relation

The most defensible current position is:

> Law 1 is the foundational law, and Law 3 is a refinement-layer law that explains why rescue remains non-universal once the local-to-global gap is present.

This is the best current structural relation map.

---

## 6. Why This Matters

This matters because it means the theory is not just accumulating separate laws.

Instead, the program may already be organizing itself into levels:

### Level 1 — Failure foundations
- local competence does not guarantee global robustness

### Level 2 — Rescue consequences
- once failure is heterogeneous, rescue mechanisms become family-sensitive

This is the beginning of a true theoretical hierarchy.

---

## 7. What This Does NOT Yet Establish

This map does not yet prove:

- that Law 1 and Law 3 are fully ordered in a final hierarchy
- that no future law will sit above them
- that Law 3 can always be derived from Law 1 in every domain
- or that the law structure is complete

It provides a first structural interpretation only.

---

## 8. Most Meaningful Next Step

The next important question is:

> Is there a candidate law that sits above both Law 1 and Law 3?

For example:
- a law about heterogeneous compositional structure itself
- a law about why rescue and failure both become family-sensitive once local-to-global scaling fails

This would be the natural next theoretical frontier.
