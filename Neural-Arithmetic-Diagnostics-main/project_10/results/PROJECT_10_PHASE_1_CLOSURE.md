# PROJECT 10 PHASE 1 CLOSURE
## From Law Extraction to Post-Adversarial Revision

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** PHASE 1 COMPLETE

---

## 1. Purpose

This document closes the first major phase of Project 10.

Phase 1 began with the launch of Project 10 as a theory-building effort and ended after:
- initial law extraction
- structured law testing
- higher-order candidate construction
- adversarial falsification pressure
- and post-adversarial theoretical revision

This document records what Phase 1 established.

---

## 2. What Phase 1 Set Out to Do

Project 10 was launched to move the broader research program from:
- project-level findings
toward
- explicit compositional failure laws

The core goals were:

1. extract candidate laws from Projects 4–9
2. test them structurally
3. avoid pseudo-theory
4. move toward bounded, falsifiable principles

These goals were substantially achieved.

---

## 3. Major Achievements of Phase 1

### A. Framework creation
Project 10 established a clear theory-building framework and law criteria.

### B. Method correction
The project moved from weak textual matching to stronger structured evidence matrices.

### C. Strong law-level results
Two major laws achieved strong support:
- Law 1
- Law 3

### D. Theory structuring
The project moved beyond isolated laws into:
- synthesis
- relation mapping
- and higher-order candidate formulation

### E. Adversarial discipline
The project then subjected the higher-order candidate to adversarial pressure rather than protecting it.

This is one of the most important achievements of Phase 1.

## 5. What Phase 1 Learned

Phase 1 learned that:

1. internal convergence is not enough for higher-order theory
2. law-level support can be strong at the foundational level
3. higher-order claims require much harder falsification discipline
4. adversarial pressure improves theory even when it weakens claims
5. bounded revision is scientifically better than decorative universality

This is the central intellectual outcome of Phase 1.

---

## 6. Final Status at End of Phase 1

At the close of Phase 1:

- **Law 1** = strongly supported
- **Law 3** = strongly supported, but bounded
- **Original higher-order candidate** = weakened
- **Revised higher-order candidate V1** = active but unverified

Project 10 therefore ends Phase 1 in a stronger scientific position than it began:
not because every claim survived unchanged,
but because the surviving structure is now more honest and more defensible.
