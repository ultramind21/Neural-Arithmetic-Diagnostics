# PROJECT 10 REGIME SPACE REFORMULATION V1
## From Higher-Order Candidate to Regime-Dependent Theory

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** ACTIVE THEORY REFORMULATION

---

## 1. Why This Reformulation Exists

Project 10 originally attempted to build a higher-order candidate law linking:

- local competence saturation without global robustness
- family-level heterogeneity
- and the need for family-aligned rescue

However, Phase 2 threshold testing showed that even the revised higher-order candidate survives only in a narrow region of the tested space.

This means the theory should no longer be formulated primarily as a single broad higher-order law.

A stronger formulation is now needed.

---

## 2. The New Theoretical Direction

The strongest emerging higher-level insight is now:

> rescue behavior is regime-dependent.

In particular, comparative rescue outcomes depend on at least:

- the level of family-level heterogeneity
- the strength of universal rescue power

This is the beginning of a regime-space theory rather than a necessity-style higher-order law.

---

## 3. Core Reformulation

### New working statement
**Family-aware rescue advantage is not globally necessary or globally stable; it emerges, shrinks, or disappears depending on the system's position in a regime space defined at least by heterogeneity level and universal rescue power.**

This is the new central reformulation.

---

## 4. What This Explains Better

This reformulation explains all current Phase 2 results more naturally:

### Low heterogeneity + baseline power
- boundary / near-tie

### Medium or high heterogeneity + baseline power
- family-aware advantage

### Moderate or strong universal power
- universal dominance across the matrix

This is much cleaner than trying to force all results into one necessity-style candidate.

---

## 5. Why This Is Stronger

This reformulation is stronger because it:

- fits the matrix results directly
- preserves the adversarial lessons honestly
- avoids overstating what the current evidence supports
- and turns Project 10 toward threshold structure instead of decorative generality

This is the correct theoretical upgrade.

---

## 6. Relation to Earlier Results

This does not erase earlier results.

### Law 1 remains foundational
Local competence is not sufficient for global robustness.

### Law 3 remains real but bounded
Family-sensitive rescue can outperform universal rescue in important regimes.

### Higher-order candidate becomes transformed
Instead of a broad law, it becomes a regime-space hypothesis.

This is a refinement, not a collapse.

---

## 7. Current Best Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation now is:

> the higher-level structure of rescue behavior is best described not by a single necessity-style law, but by a bounded regime space in which heterogeneity and universal rescue power jointly determine whether family-aware rescue wins, ties, or loses.

This is the best current higher-level statement in Project 10.

---

## 8. What This Suggests for Future Work

Future work should now ask:

- where exactly are the threshold boundaries?
- what additional axes belong in the regime space?
- is there a compact phase diagram for rescue behavior?
- and can other candidate laws be reformulated similarly if they resist simple law-like compression?

This opens a stronger Phase 2 direction.
