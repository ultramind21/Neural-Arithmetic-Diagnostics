# PROJECT 10 PHASE 2 THRESHOLD MATRIX RESULTS

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** COMPLETE  
**Verdict:** REVISED CANDIDATE HOLDS ONLY IN A NARROW REGION

---

## 1. Main Result

The first Phase 2 threshold matrix showed the following verdict distribution:

- supports revised candidate: 2
- boundary / mixed: 1
- weakens revised candidate: 6

This is the most important landscape-level result so far in Phase 2.

---

## 2. What This Means

The revised higher-order candidate V1 does not hold broadly across the tested regime space.

Instead, it holds only in a narrow region:

- baseline universal rescue power
- and medium or high heterogeneity

Outside that region, the candidate weakens.

This means the revised candidate is still too broad in its current formulation.

---

## 3. Most Important Structural Finding

The strongest structural finding is:

> higher-level rescue behavior appears to depend on position in a regime space defined by heterogeneity level and universal rescue power.

This is stronger and more defensible than a broad necessity-style higher-order law.

---

## 4. Why This Matters

This result changes the theory direction of Project 10.

The project should no longer treat the revised higher-order candidate as if it were close to a stable law.

Instead, the stronger emerging insight is that:

- family-aware advantage is regime-dependent
- universal rescue dominance is regime-dependent
- and theory may need to be expressed as a map or threshold structure rather than a single high-level necessity statement

This is a major theoretical shift.

---

## 5. Correct Interpretation

The strongest safe interpretation is:

> the revised higher-order candidate V1 survives only in a limited region of the tested matrix, and the strongest higher-level insight now appears to be regime-dependent rather than necessity-based.

This is the correct current position.
