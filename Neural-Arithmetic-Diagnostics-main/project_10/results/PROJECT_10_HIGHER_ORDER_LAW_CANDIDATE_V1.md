# PROJECT 10 HIGHER-ORDER LAW CANDIDATE V1
## First Candidate Above Law 1 and Law 3

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** ACTIVE HIGHER-ORDER LAW CANDIDATE

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the first higher-order law candidate in Project 10.

The purpose is to test whether the currently supported laws can be organized under a more general principle.

At present, Project 10 already has:

- Law 1: local competence is not sufficient for global robustness
- Law 3: rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal

The question now is:

> Is there a more general law that explains why both of these are true?

---

## 2. Candidate Higher-Order Law

### Working statement
**When local competence reaches saturation without global robustness, successful rescue requires mechanisms aligned to the heterogeneous structure of family-level failure.**

This is the first higher-order law candidate.

---

## 3. What This Candidate Is Trying to Unify

### Law 1
Local competence can become strong without yielding global robust composition.

### Law 3
Rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal.

The higher-order idea is that these are not unrelated facts.

Instead:

- Law 1 identifies the existence of the local-to-global gap
- Law 3 identifies the structure of rescue once that gap is present
- the higher-order law says both emerge because family-level failure is structurally heterogeneous and therefore requires heterogeneous support

---

## 4. Why This Candidate Is Plausible

This candidate is plausible because the current research line repeatedly shows the same pattern:

1. local competence becomes real
2. global robustness still fails
3. failure is not uniform across families
4. rescue does not apply uniformly across families
5. architecture must become family-sensitive to succeed

This makes the higher-order law a natural synthesis candidate.

---

## 5. What It Predicts

If this higher-order law is true, then the following should tend to occur:

- strong local competence without guaranteed global success
- heterogeneous family-level failure
- rescue mechanisms that must be matched to family structure
- architecture-level gains that are strongest when support mechanisms are differentiated rather than universal

These are all already visible in the current program.

---

## 6. What Would Count Against It

The candidate would be weakened or falsified by evidence such as:

- a system with strong local competence that also shows broad global robustness without needing heterogeneous support
- a genuinely universal rescue mechanism that works broadly across families despite strong family differences
- evidence that family heterogeneity is superficial rather than structurally important

These would all count strongly against the candidate.

---

## 7. Current Best Interpretation

The strongest current safe interpretation is:

> Law 1 and Law 3 may be instances of a deeper regularity: once local competence saturates without broad global robustness, family-level rescue becomes a heterogeneous architecture problem rather than a universal patch problem.

This is the current best higher-order formulation.

---

## 8. Why This Matters

This matters because it raises the theory layer above isolated laws.

Without a higher-order candidate:
- Project 10 risks becoming a catalog of supported statements

With a higher-order candidate:
- the project begins to ask whether the whole research line is converging on a compact explanatory principle

That is a major theoretical step.

---

## 9. What This Does NOT Yet Establish

This candidate does not yet establish:

- a final higher-order law
- a completed law hierarchy
- or a full top-level theory of the program

It is a candidate only.

Its value lies in giving the next law-testing step a much sharper target.

---

The next step is:

- and determine whether it behaves more like:
  - a true higher-order law
  - or only a useful synthesis statement
