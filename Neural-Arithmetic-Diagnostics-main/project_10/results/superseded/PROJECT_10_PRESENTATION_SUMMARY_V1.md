# PROJECT 10 PRESENTATION SUMMARY V1
## Compositional Failure Laws to Regime-Space Rescue Theory

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** PRESENTATION SUMMARY

---

## 1. What Project 10 Tried to Do

Project 10 began as a theory-building project.

Its goal was to move beyond:
- strong project-level findings
- and isolated empirical patterns

toward:
- explicit compositional failure laws
- and, if possible, a higher-order theory tying them together

---

## 2. What It Established Early

Project 10 quickly established two strong law-level results:

### Law 1
**Local competence is not sufficient for global robustness.**

### Law 3
**Rescue mechanisms are family-sensitive rather than universal.**

These became the two strongest early laws of the project.

---

## 3. What Happened Next

Project 10 then attempted a stronger move:

- connect Law 1 and Law 3 through a higher-order candidate
- test that candidate directly
- and see whether a broader top-level principle could survive falsification

This is where the project became much more interesting.

---

## 4. What the Adversarial Phase Revealed

The adversarial phase showed that the original higher-order candidate was too strong.

It did not survive cleanly in its necessity-style form.

A narrower revision was attempted,
but Phase 2 testing showed that even the revised version was still too broad as a flat principle.

This was not a failure of the project.

It was the project working correctly.

---

## 5. The Strongest Final Shift

The strongest final shift in Project 10 was this:

> the highest-level structure of rescue behavior is better described by a threshold-structured regime space than by one flat higher-order law.

This became the central theoretical outcome.

---

## 6. What Replaced the Original Higher-Order Ambition

Instead of one broad law,
Project 10 ended up with a stronger and more honest theory structure:

- Law 1 remained foundational
- Law 3 remained real but bounded
- rescue behavior became regime-dependent
- threshold regions became visible
- and the rescue boundary itself appeared to shift with heterogeneity

This is the final shape of the project.

---

## 7. Why This Matters

Project 10 matters because it did not force a beautiful but unstable top-level theory to survive.

Instead, it allowed adversarial pressure to improve the theory.

The result is a stronger scientific outcome:

- fewer decorative claims
- more explicit boundaries
- more honest theory
- and a richer account of rescue behavior

This is exactly what serious theory-building should do.

---

## 8. Final Project 10 Position

The strongest final Project 10 position is:

> compositional rescue behavior is best understood through a threshold-structured regime space in which family-aware advantage, mixed behavior, and universal dominance occupy different regions, and the boundary between them shifts with heterogeneity.

This is the best final summary of the project.
