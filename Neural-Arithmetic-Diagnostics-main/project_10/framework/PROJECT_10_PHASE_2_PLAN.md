# PROJECT 10 PHASE 2 PLAN
## Revised Higher-Order Testing

**Date:** April 2026  
**Project:** 10  
**Status:** PHASE 2 ACTIVE

---

## 1. Why Phase 2 Exists

Phase 1 of Project 10 established:
- a law-testing framework
- strong support for Law 1
- strong but bounded support for Law 3
- weakening of the original higher-order candidate
- and a narrower revised higher-order candidate

This creates a clear Phase 2 task:

> test the revised higher-order candidate directly, rather than returning immediately to broader law expansion.

---

## 2. Core Goal of Phase 2

The core goal of Phase 2 is:

> determine whether the revised higher-order candidate survives structured testing as a bounded comparative principle.

The project is no longer asking whether family-aligned rescue is strictly necessary.

It is now asking whether:

- family-aligned rescue tends to outperform universal rescue
- under substantive heterogeneity
- when universal mechanism power is not sufficient to erase that advantage

This is the central Phase 2 question.

---

## 3. Revised Higher-Order Candidate

The active candidate entering Phase 2 is:

> When local competence saturates without global robustness, family-aligned rescue tends to outperform universal rescue when family-level failure heterogeneity is substantive and universal mechanism power is not sufficient to override that heterogeneity.

This is the Phase 2 target.

---

## 4. What Phase 2 Must Do Better

Phase 2 must improve on Phase 1 by:

- testing narrower claims rather than broad necessity claims
- making boundary conditions central rather than secondary
- treating comparative advantage and strict necessity as different things
- and explicitly tracking where the revised candidate stops applying

This is a more mature theory phase.

---

## 5. Main Testing Axes

Phase 2 should focus on three main axes:

### A. Heterogeneity threshold
How much family-level heterogeneity is enough for family-aware rescue to regain a meaningful advantage?

### B. Power threshold
At what universal-rescue strength does family-aware advantage shrink, disappear, or reverse?

### C. Comparative regime map
What combinations of heterogeneity and mechanism power support:
- family-aware advantage
- near-tie
- or universal dominance?

These three axes define the main Phase 2 landscape.
